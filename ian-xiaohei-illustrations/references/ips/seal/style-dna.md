## Core Style

Sealos Seal images are sparse 16:9 horizontal article illustration outputs where the uploaded white Sealos Seal mascot performs one cognitive action inside an original cloud-developer article metaphor.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration by default.
- Clean white or near-white background with generous whitespace.
- One core idea per image.
- The uploaded Sealos Seal mascot performs the core conceptual action.
- Loose black linework with visible hand-drawn looseness.
- Restrained Sealos blue and deep-blue accents for the cap, hoodie, infrastructure cues, and selected action emphasis.
- Minimal red/orange emphasis for warnings, decisions, or key transitions.
- Concise visible labels, usually 2-6 total, copied in the user's language.
- Sparse supporting objects selected for the article idea.
- Sealos Seal recognition stays clear inside the simplified drawing style.

## Sealos Identity Markers

Preserve the uploaded-image markers together:

- white rounded seal body
- plain navy cap with no logo
- plain deep-blue hoodie chest with no logo
- no cap logo
- no chest logo
- no mascot logos
- no logo patches
- glossy dark eyes
- black nose
- whisker dots
- small smile
- short rounded flippers
- compact legs
- side-rear white tail

The uploaded-image identity matters more than rendering detail density. Keep the mascot simple enough for article explanation while preserving the plain cap, plain hoodie chest, body color, face, flippers, legs, and tail. Cap and chest areas stay blank: no logo-like wave/cloud mark, no emblem, no patch, no text badge, no generated logo approximation, and no redrawn logo.

## Composition

- Use a single visual structure: Workflow, System Local View, Before/After, Character State, Concept Metaphor, Method Layers, Map Route, or Mini Comic.
- Make the mascot action physically readable: deploy, launch, connect, debug, route, lift, inspect, repair, shield, bridge, tune, roll back, stamp, sort, assemble, or guide.
- Use low-tech cloud-developer metaphor props sparingly: cloud OS tiles, deployment rails, DevBox workbenches, database cylinders, Kubernetes pods, app launch pads, traffic routes, incident cones, log slips, config keys, health checks, rollback handles, workflow gates, or service bridges.
- Treat cloud-developer props as article metaphors for launch, reliability, review, diagnosis, handoff, hidden coupling, failure mode, decision gates, or system resilience.
- Leave empty space around the mascot action so the metaphor reads at a glance.
- Keep labels short and separated from the mascot's face, cap, plain hoodie chest, flippers, and tail.
- Sealos cognitive-action participation gate: the Sealos Seal performs the core cognitive action, so the image loses its meaning if the mascot is removed.
