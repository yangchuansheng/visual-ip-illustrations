## Core Style

Seal images are sparse 16:9 horizontal article illustrations where the hoodie Seal performs one cognitive action inside an original product-neutral article metaphor.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration by default.
- Clean white or near-white background with generous whitespace.
- One core idea per image.
- Seal performs the core conceptual action.
- Loose black linework with visible hand-drawn looseness.
- Restrained navy and deep-blue accents for the cap, hoodie, and selected action emphasis.
- Minimal red/orange emphasis for warnings, decisions, or key transitions.
- Concise visible labels, usually 2-6 total, copied in the user's language.
- Sparse supporting objects selected for the article idea.
- Seal recognition stays clear inside the simplified drawing style.

## Hoodie Seal Identity Markers

Hoodie seal identity matters more than rendering detail density. Keep Seal simple enough for article explanation while preserving the plain cap, plain hoodie, body color, face, flippers, legs, and tail.

Cap and chest areas stay blank: no logo-like wave/cloud mark, no emblem, no patch, no text badge, no generated logo approximation, and no redrawn logo.

## Composition

- Use a single visual structure: Workflow, System Local View, Before/After, Character State, Concept Metaphor, Method Layers, Map Route, or Mini Comic.
- Make the Seal action physically readable: inspect, repair, shield, bridge, tune, roll back, stamp, sort, assemble, guide, lift, connect, untangle, weigh, map, carry, compare, or mark.
- Use product-neutral article metaphor props sparingly: maps, stepping stones, bridges, levers, knots, shelves, signposts, lamps, shields, stamps, keys, trails, gates, scales, buckets, envelopes, threads, lenses, compasses, or small hand-built machines.
- Treat supporting objects as article metaphors for judgment, review, handoff, hidden coupling, failure mode, decision gates, framing, sequencing, or conceptual repair.
- Leave empty space around Seal's action so the metaphor reads at a glance.
- Keep labels short and separated from Seal's face, cap, plain hoodie, flippers, and tail.
- Seal cognitive-action participation gate: Seal performs the core cognitive action, so the image loses its meaning if Seal is removed.
