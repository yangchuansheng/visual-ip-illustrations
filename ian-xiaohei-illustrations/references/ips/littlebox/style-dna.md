# Littlebox Style DNA

Littlebox images are sparse 16:9 article illustrations where a closed paper box performs one abstract operation through rough marker drawing.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration by default.
- One flat background per image: pale sky blue `#E3F2FD` or pale lavender `#E6E6FA`.
- Background balance across a set: even counts use equal sky-blue and lavender counts; odd counts differ by at most one unless article order gives a stronger reason.
- Black `#000000` as the dominant line, object, character, and label color.
- Thick rough black marker linework with chunky brush-pen weight.
- Dry-brush texture, tiny ink gaps, organic rough edges, and natural line variation.
- Near-white Littlebox body, kept as white or near-white negative space on the pale background.
- Sparse amber `#F59E0B` and coral `#E85D75` accents.
- Generous whitespace. The main Littlebox subject usually occupies 35%-55% of the canvas.
- One core idea per image.
- Littlebox performs the conceptual action.
- Sparse visible labels, usually 3-6 total.

## Palette

- **Black `#000000`**: outlines, objects, labels, character, and most structure.
- **Pale sky blue `#E3F2FD`**: technical, structural, systems, clarity, tools, interfaces, and method explanations.
- **Pale lavender `#E6E6FA`**: reflective, creative, emotional, abstract, exploratory, uncertain, or narrative concepts.
- **Amber `#F59E0B`**: Littlebox's seam tape and jagged front tab first; flow paths, motion curves, or useful arrows only when separated from identity tape.
- **Coral `#E85D75`**: warning, stamp, error, result, or decisive emphasis.

Use accents sparingly. A strong image can use black plus one accent.

## Background Rules

- For one image, choose `#E3F2FD` or `#E6E6FA` by theme.
- For multiple images, assign backgrounds during planning before prompt writing.
- Keep background counts balanced across the full set.
- Avoid long runs of one background unless the article sequence needs that rhythm.
- Use one flat background color per image.

## Texture And Labels

Use prompt phrases such as:

- `minimalist hand-drawn illustration`
- `bold marker illustration`
- `chunky brush-pen style`
- `fat-tipped black marker lines, not thin pen lines`
- `very thick black outlines`
- `dry brush texture with tiny gaps in ink coverage`
- `organic rough edges`
- `natural line weight variation`
- `imperfect boxes and wobbly folds`
- `warm handmade feel`
- `handwritten marker labels, not typed text`

Visible labels should feel drawn with the same rough black marker as the illustration. CJK labels should look handmade, uneven, and integrated with the drawing.

## Visual Vetoes

Regenerate or edit when the result becomes:

- open box, half-open box, raised flaps, cutaway, or visible box interior
- glossy mascot art
- cute sticker pack
- logistics logo or delivery-company icon
- realistic cardboard rendering
- tan, kraft brown, beige, or shaded cardboard body
- polished vector infographic
- thin-line icon art
- clean geometric box icon
- auto-traced, thresholded, or posterized bitmap look
- clean digital font labels
- formal PPT diagram or dense course slide
- children's book scene
- 3D object render
- shadows, gradients, paper texture, grainy background, or vignette
- complex UI screen
- too many arrows, nodes, labels, or panels
- top-left diagram title such as `Workflow`, `System`, `Roadmap`, or `Framework`

Taste target: the image should feel like a clever product thinker drew a strange box on a page to explain the one thing that matters.
