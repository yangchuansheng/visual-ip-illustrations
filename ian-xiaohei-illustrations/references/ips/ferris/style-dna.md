# Ferris Style DNA

Ferris route status: `source-reviewed`.
Ferris output path: `assets/<article-slug>-ferris/`.
Ferris source/trademark authority: `source.md`.
Source/trademark note: Ferris is a `source-reviewed` Rust-community mascot route. Source context lives in `source.md`; Rust/Cargo marks, logos, official-affiliation claims, and endorsement wording follow the Rust Foundation trademark policy context recorded in `source.md`.
Public rendered Ferris samples remain gated by `RELEASE_CHECKLIST.md`.
Ferris route block: generic-crab drift, passive Ferris placement, Rust-logo misuse, official-affiliation cues, over-detailed mascot rendering, excessive text, source-asset tracing, and route leakage all fail the route.
Save accepted Ferris output under `assets/<article-slug>-ferris/` with an ordered English slug filename such as `01-topic-name.png`.

## Core Style

Ferris images are sparse 16:9 article illustrations where Ferris performs one cognitive action inside an original Rust-themed article metaphor.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration by default.
- Clean white or near-white page space with generous whitespace.
- One core idea per image.
- Ferris performs the core conceptual action.
- Rough hand-drawn black linework with visible human looseness.
- Limited orange or rust-red accents for Ferris and selected action emphasis.
- Concise visible labels, usually 2-6 total, copied in the user's language.
- Sparse supporting objects selected for the article idea.
- Ferris crab-mascot recognition stays clear inside the simplified drawing style.

## Ferris Recognition Cues

Preserve several cues together:

- compact orange or rust-red crab body
- broad crab shell silhouette
- two visible claws
- small legs
- two eyes or eyestalk eyes
- active body language tied to the concept
- focused, guarding, sorting, type-checking, borrowing, compiling, inspecting, untangling, bracing, stamping, or releasing state

Ferris identity recognizability matters more than detail density. Keep Ferris simple enough for article explanation while preserving route-specific crab-mascot recognition.

## Composition

- Use a single visual structure: workflow, system local view, before-after, character state, concept metaphor, method layers, map route, or mini comic.
- Make the action physically readable: clamping, sorting, guarding, borrowing, returning, stamping, carrying, bridging, untangling, locking, inspecting, rerouting, balancing, or releasing.
- Use low-tech Rust-themed metaphor props sparingly: ownership tags, borrow ropes, lifetime clocks, compiler gates, type-check stamps, safety nets, unsafe warning flags, trait stamps, dependency knots, crate stacks, release locks, or review gates.
- Treat Rust-themed props as article metaphors for trust, proof, safety, review, dependency, handoff, hidden cost, failure mode, decision gate, or system resilience.
- Leave empty space around Ferris's action so the metaphor reads at a glance.
- Keep labels short and separated from Ferris's eyes, claws, legs, and shell silhouette.
- Ferris cognitive-action participation gate: Ferris performs the core cognitive action, so the image loses its meaning if Ferris is removed.

## Ferris Visual Vetoes

Regenerate or edit when the result contains:

- generic-crab drift
- passive Ferris placement
- Rust-logo misuse
- official-affiliation cues
- over-detailed mascot rendering
- excessive text
- source-asset tracing
- route leakage
- dense explanatory text
- clean digital font labels
- formal PPT diagram or dense course slide
- crowded nodes, arrows, labels, or panels
- official Rust diagram layout
- IDE, terminal, or UI screenshot scene
- source asset pose copying
- logo-forward composition
- beach crab, seafood icon, random red animal, or route-neutral crab mascot

## Stable Gates

- Ferris identity recognizability gate: Ferris stays recognizable through compact orange or rust-red crab body, broad crab shell silhouette, two visible claws, small legs, two eyes or eyestalk eyes, and active body language.
- Ferris cognitive-action participation gate: Ferris performs the core cognitive action, so the image loses its meaning if Ferris is removed.
- Ferris source-asset tracing gate: the scene is an original article metaphor and avoids traced source assets, public mascot asset sheets, source-pose copying, and official logo arrangements.
- Ferris generic-crab drift gate: a route-neutral crab, seafood icon, beach crab, or anonymous red mascot fails the Ferris route.
- Ferris trademark-boundary gate: Rust/Cargo marks, Rust logos, official-affiliation cues, endorsement wording, and logo-forward compositions require repair or release review.
- Ferris route block: generic-crab drift, passive Ferris placement, Rust-logo misuse, official-affiliation cues, over-detailed mascot rendering, excessive text, source-asset tracing, and route leakage all fail the route.
