# Tom IP

Tom route status: `gated-authorized`.

Rights-status note: Tom is a protected-character route. Use `rights.md` as the authority for authorization scope, distribution boundary, public-sample policy, reviewer fields, and any status change.

## Core Identity

Tom is Tom from Tom and Jerry under a protected-character route.

Phase 7 defines a solo Tom article-illustration variant: Tom appears alone as the active operator of an article concept. The image uses original explanatory scenes and fresh metaphors for article-body illustration.

## Recognition Rules

Preserve recognizable Tom cues in a simplified article-illustration style:

- grey or blue-grey cat body
- white muzzle
- white belly areas
- expressive ears
- long tail
- visible paws
- Tom-like head shape and body silhouette
- alert, focused, startled, guarding, chasing, repairing, sorting, catching, or stamping body language

For final article images, preserve enough cues that Tom remains recognizable as Tom while the composition stays sparse and explanatory.

## Cognitive Action Responsibility

Tom must perform the central cognitive action in every Tom-route illustration.

Use Tom to:

- pull evidence into order
- chase an idea
- operate a lever
- guard a gate
- catch evidence
- hold back chaos
- bridge a gap
- sort signals
- repair a broken flow
- stamp a result
- trap a recurring problem
- redirect a confusing path
- carry a critical decision across a gap

Tom cognitive-action participation gate: the visual metaphor depends on Tom's action.

## Solo Route Boundary

- The Phase 7 Tom route is solo Tom.
- Supporting objects come from article metaphors: evidence slips, traps, levers, doors, paths, tools, piles, bridges, stamps, broken machines, gates, tags, ropes, springs, nets, or warning marks.
- Scene design stays original, article-explanatory, and route-local.
- Broader cast, logo, title-card, and source-frame concepts require separate authorization scope and later review.

## Failure Modes

Regenerate or rewrite when the result contains:

- generic cat
- passive Tom
- default Jerry
- broader cast
- logo or title card
- source recreation
- removable character
- generic-cat drift
- source-frame recreation
- broad availability wording
- dense franchise scene
- Tom standing beside the concept while props carry the meaning

## Stable Gates

- Tom identity recognizability gate: the image preserves Tom-specific grey or blue-grey body, white muzzle, white belly areas, expressive ears, tail, paws, and Tom-like silhouette.
- Tom cognitive-action participation gate: Tom performs the central cognitive action, and removing Tom breaks the metaphor.
- Tom source-frame recreation gate: the scene avoids copied show frames, logos, title cards, and recognizable episode staging.
- Tom generic-cat drift gate: an anonymous cat or route-neutral animal fails the Tom route.
