# Tom Style DNA

Tom route status: `gated-authorized`.

Rights-status note: Tom is a protected-character route. Use `rights.md` as the authority for authorization scope, distribution boundary, public-sample policy, reviewer fields, and any status change.

## Core Style

Tom images are sparse 16:9 article illustrations where Tom performs one cognitive action inside an original explanatory metaphor.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration by default.
- Clean white or near-white page space with generous whitespace.
- One core idea per image.
- Tom performs the core conceptual action.
- Rough hand-drawn linework with visible human looseness.
- Concise visible labels, usually 2-6 total, copied in the user's language.
- Sparse supporting objects selected for the article idea.
- Tom-like silhouette stays recognizable inside the simplified drawing style.

## Tom Recognition Cues

Preserve several cues together:

- grey or blue-grey cat body
- white muzzle
- white belly areas
- expressive ears
- long tail
- visible paws
- Tom-like head shape and body silhouette
- alert, focused, frustrated, guarding, chasing, sorting, repairing, or decisive state

Tom identity recognizability matters more than detail density. Keep Tom simple enough for article explanation while preserving the protected-character route identity.

## Composition

- Use a single visual structure: workflow, system local view, before-after, character state, concept metaphor, method layers, map route, or mini comic.
- Make the action physically readable: pulling, catching, guarding, repairing, bridging, sorting, stamping, or operating.
- Keep support props small and purposeful: evidence slips, levers, gates, paths, tags, piles, traps, nets, broken machines, bridges, stamps, tools, or warning marks.
- Leave empty space around Tom's action so the metaphor reads at a glance.
- Keep labels short and separated from Tom's face and paws.

## Protected-Route Visual Vetoes

Regenerate or edit when the result contains:

- source-frame recreation
- show logos
- title cards
- default Jerry usage
- broad cast expansion
- generic-cat drift
- excessive text
- route leakage
- dense explanatory text
- broad availability wording
- franchise-poster layout
- clean digital font labels
- formal PPT diagram or dense course slide
- crowded nodes, arrows, labels, or panels
- realistic cartoon still rendering

## Stable Gates

- Tom identity recognizability gate: Tom stays recognizable through grey or blue-grey body, white muzzle, white belly areas, expressive ears, tail, paws, and Tom-like silhouette.
- Tom cognitive-action participation gate: Tom performs the core cognitive action, so the image loses its meaning if Tom is removed.
- Tom source-frame recreation gate: the scene is an original article metaphor and avoids copied show frames, title cards, logos, and recognizable episode staging.
- Tom generic-cat drift gate: a vague grey cat, ordinary mascot cat, or route-neutral animal fails the Tom route.
- Tom protected-route block: source-frame recreation, show logos, title cards, default Jerry usage, broad cast expansion, generic-cat drift, excessive text, and route leakage all fail the route.
