## Composition Families

### Workflow

Use for input-to-output reasoning, editorial handoffs, review loops, learning sequences, decision paths, repair flows, and method steps.

Draw a loose left-to-right or curved path. OpenClaw operates the decisive handoff point: gripping a loose thread, connecting stepping stones, stamping a checkpoint, repairing a bridge, sorting envelopes, or marking the useful turn.

### System Local View

Use for filters, decision points, hidden dependencies, evidence clusters, priority shelves, comparison layers, and resilience checks.

Draw only 3-5 core parts. OpenClaw works inside one decisive part: inspecting a lens, untangling threads, weighing claims on scales, shielding a fragile idea, tuning a lamp, or repairing a lever.

### Before/After

Use for vague-to-clear, tangled-to-readable, scattered-to-sorted, fragile-to-stable, overloaded-to-focused, and stuck-to-moving changes.

Left side is unstable. Right side is calmer. OpenClaw performs the transformation in the center with claws, a knot, bridge, map, gate, shelf, scale, stamp, or small hand-built machine.

### Character State

Use for focused review, careful comparison, overloaded sorting, guarded judgment, stuck reasoning, and confident handoff.

Show OpenClaw in 2-4 compact states. Each state has one short label and one physical action: inspect, grip, sort, bridge, tune, stamp, carry, map, connect, shield, compare, mark, untangle, weigh, guide, assemble, or repair.

### Concept Metaphor

Use for judgment scales, trust bridges, decision gates, tangled threads, map routes, evidence shelves, review lamps, hidden keys, and small strange machines.

Build one memorable low-tech object or small hand-built machine. OpenClaw is the operator of that object, with claw position, antennae direction, pupils, legs, and body direction tied to the action.

### Method Layers

Use for layered methods, capability stacks, reasoning ladders, evidence shelves, review depth, confidence building, and staged handoffs.

Draw stacked boxes, shelves, steps, or loose construction layers. OpenClaw carries, shields, stamps, sorts, tunes, repairs, weighs, grips, or bridges one layer so the method becomes usable.

### Map Route

Use for idea paths, reader journeys, publishing routes, migration paths, decision trails, and problem-resolution routes.

Draw one curved route with a few nodes. OpenClaw walks, guides, bridges, reroutes, repairs, stamps, marks, compares, grips, or maps the decisive path segment.

### Mini Comic

Use for failure-to-fix sequences, repeated reasoning mistakes, handoff stories, comparison lessons, and small process stories.

Use 2-3 loose panels. Each panel shows one OpenClaw action. Keep OpenClaw consistent, active, source-reviewed, and article-explanatory across panels.

## Metaphor Invention Rule

OpenClaw composition original-metaphor gate: invent the scene from the current article idea, then choose the OpenClaw action that makes the idea readable.

## Supporting Object Pool

Use small, physical objects that make article logic visible:

- maps, folded routes, dotted paths, and signposts
- stepping stones, bridges, gates, shelves, and ladders
- knots, threads, ropes, hooks, clips, and loose wires
- lamps, lenses, compasses, scales, stamps, keys, buckets, envelopes, and cards
- shields, levers, pulleys, tiny cranes, simple switches, and hand-built machines
- small notes, source tags, checklist tags, review marks, and route labels

Keep the pool sparse. Use 3-6 supporting objects for most images.

## OpenClaw Action Patterns

- Claw grip: OpenClaw grips one loose idea, thread, key, or bridge piece to show control.
- Antenna scan: OpenClaw leans antennae toward a hidden signal, risky gap, or source note.
- Cyan focus: cyan pupils point directly at the decision point, evidence, or path turn.
- Red body anchor: the round red body anchors the composition without becoming a poster logo.
- Short-leg movement: short legs show a simple walk, climb, or stance near the decisive object.
- Two-claw bridge: side claw-like arms hold two parts together to show handoff, trust, or repair.
