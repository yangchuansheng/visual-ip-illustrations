# Littlebox QA Checklist

Use this gate before delivery, regeneration, or local text repair.

## Pass Criteria

- Image is 16:9 horizontal unless the user requested another shape.
- Background is clean flat `#E3F2FD` or `#E6E6FA`.
- Multi-image sets maintain background balance between sky blue and lavender.
- Littlebox appears and is recognizable.
- Littlebox uses the canonical front-left three-quarter model.
- Littlebox is closed: no open lid, half-open lid, raised flaps, cutaway, or visible interior.
- Eyes are on the front panel.
- Tiny legs attach under the front panel.
- Body is white or near-white, not tan, brown, beige, or realistic cardboard.
- Body remains compact and squat.
- Outlines are thick rough dry-brush marker lines, not thin vector strokes.
- Linework and labels share the same rough hand-drawn marker style.
- Littlebox is the active operator of the concept.
- When acting, Littlebox uses side-seam twig arms, with at most two arms total.
- The amber identity tape follows the central closed-lid seam.
- The tape is narrow and has a jagged torn front tab when the front lip is visible.
- The image has one core idea.
- Main subject occupies about 35%-55% of the canvas.
- Elements are distributed across both axes.
- Visible labels are short, usually 3-6 total.
- Labels use the user's language and look like rough handwritten marker text.
- Amber is limited to tape, paths, motion, or useful flow.
- Flow arrows or paths are amber and lighter than the black character outline.
- Coral is limited to warning, stamp, result, or decisive emphasis.

## Fail Signals

Regenerate or edit if:

- Littlebox is passive or removable.
- side-seam twig arms are missing in an action scene.
- Littlebox has extra arms, a lower spare arm, a tail-like helper line, or any extra limb-like stroke.
- Arms emerge from the face, lid, eyes, front panel, or prop instead of side seams.
- Arms become thick mascot hands, gloves, fingers, or sleeves.
- The tape jumps to a side wall, front face, random prop, unrelated flap, or back panel.
- Multiple unrelated amber tape patches appear on Littlebox.
- The tape is too wide, reads like a whole orange flap, or misses the jagged torn front tab.
- Any wrong tape placement or missing jagged front tab breaks the Littlebox identity.
- The front tape tab becomes a smooth rectangle.
- Littlebox is open, half-open, cutaway, or has raised flaps.
- Any visible interior appears.
- The box reads as a laptop, toolbox, treasure chest, pizza box, tray, bucket, crate, bag, or mailbox.
- Eyes move to the side, lid, prop, or label.
- The front-left three-quarter identity disappears.
- Body is tan, brown, beige, shaded, realistic cardboard, glossy, or 3D.
- Body becomes a long carton, bridge, press machine, tray, pipe, or generic output package.
- Linework is thin, smooth, geometric, clean vector, auto-traced, thresholded, posterized, or digitally cleaned.
- Labels use clean digital fonts, sans-serif type, bold poster type, vector text, calligraphy, or pasted-on typography.
- Labels are long, mistranslated, or unreadable.
- Too many nodes, arrows, labels, or panels appear.
- Large black arrows dominate the flow.
- The image becomes a formal process chart, dense course slide, UI screen, logistics logo, package mascot, or cute sticker.
- Background uses texture, gradients, shadows, scenic detail, or one color too heavily across a set without thematic reason.
- Accent colors dominate the black marker drawing.
- The composition depends on opening the box instead of external props and side-seam twig-arm action.

## Iteration Moves

- Too ordinary: choose a more physical Littlebox action.
- Too complex: delete objects until Littlebox plus 2-4 supports remain.
- Too cute: emphasize dot eyes, deadpan expression, no smile, no costume.
- Too diagrammatic: enlarge one action and loosen alignment.
- Text wrong: reduce label count and quote exact labels.
- State wrong: regenerate with closed-only Littlebox.
- Tape wrong: regenerate with explicit jagged front-tab seam-tape wording.
- Arms wrong: regenerate with explicit side-seam twig-arm wording.
- Arm count wrong: route extra categories through external props.
- Style drift: regenerate with rough hand-drawn marker labels and dry-brush linework.
- Body stretched: move the action into a separate prop and keep Littlebox compact.
- Flow too diagrammatic: replace black arrows with one loose amber motion path.

## Delivery Judgment

A strong Littlebox image makes the reader think: `That box is doing the idea.` If the concept still works after removing Littlebox, regenerate or rewrite the prompt.
