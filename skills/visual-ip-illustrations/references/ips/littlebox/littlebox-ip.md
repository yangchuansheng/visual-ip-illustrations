# Littlebox IP

Littlebox is a recurring closed paper-box character for article illustrations. It is a small operator that turns ideas, evidence, tasks, and systems into structure.

## Core Identity

- Littlebox is closed-only in final article illustrations.
- Default view is front-left three-quarter: the front panel is the largest visible face, one side panel is visible, and the closed top lid is visible.
- Two small dot eyes sit on the front panel, horizontally centered.
- Tiny legs attach under the front panel.
- Side-seam twig arms appear when Littlebox handles, carries, pushes, catches, sorts, points, or holds.
- Littlebox has at most two arms total: one left side-seam twig arm and one right side-seam twig arm.
- Body is white or near-white, never tan, kraft brown, beige, or realistic cardboard.
- The outline uses fat, rough, wobbly black marker strokes.
- Expression is calm and deadpan: no mouth, smile, eyebrows, blush, costume, or accessories.

## Canonical Tape

Littlebox has exactly one amber seam tape.

- Tape sits on the central closed-lid seam.
- Tape runs front-to-back across the top plane.
- Tape is narrow, about 10%-15% of the top width.
- Tape has a short amber front tab hanging slightly over the top front edge.
- The front tab has an irregular sawtooth / jagged torn front tab, usually 2-3 small teeth.
- The jagged front tab faces the viewer when the front lip is visible.
- Tape may be black-outlined when large enough.
- Tape never becomes a side-wall sticker, face-panel patch, back-panel patch, loose prop, arrow, or several unrelated tape marks.

## Canonical Lid

- Two top flaps meet along one central seam.
- The central seam runs front-to-back across the top plane, perpendicular to the front edge.
- The lid seam and jagged front tape tab remain visible when Littlebox is large enough.
- Raised flaps, open flaps, half-open lids, cutaways, visible interiors, and main openings fail the identity gate.
- Littlebox should read as a compact paper box, not a laptop, treasure chest, toolbox, pizza box, bucket, tray, shopping bag, crate, or mailbox.

## Canonical Proportions

- Front panel is slightly wider than tall.
- Side panel is narrower than the front panel.
- Eyes sit in the upper-middle front panel.
- Legs are short vertical black strokes below the front panel.
- Body is compact and squat, not a tall cube, long shipping carton, or flat tray.
- Keep the front panel readable as the character face.

## Action Vocabulary

Use Littlebox to:

- collect, catch, hold, and carry
- close, seal, and stamp
- sort, fold, compress, and bundle
- bridge, block, route, and redirect
- hide, reveal, store, and deliver
- spill, overflow, recover, and repair

Action should happen through side-seam twig arms, external slips, stacks, parcels, stamps, tags, paths, slots, or props while the box stays closed.

## Deformation Limits

- Keep the compact box body intact.
- If a concept needs compression, add a press plate, heavy slip, squeezed sheet, or output parcel.
- If a concept needs routing or handoff, use an external amber path.
- If a concept needs bridging, let Littlebox hold or place a bridge object.
- Arms, paths, arrows, and props can stretch; the box body stays compact.

## Failure Modes

Regenerate or rewrite if:

- Littlebox stands beside the idea as decoration.
- Side-seam twig arms are missing in an action scene.
- Littlebox has extra arms, wrong arm origin, lower spare arms, tail-like helper lines, thick mascot hands, fingers, gloves, or sleeves.
- The amber seam tape is missing, misplaced, too wide, duplicated, or missing the jagged torn front tab.
- Littlebox is open, half-open, cutaway, raised-flap, or shows any visible interior.
- Eyes move off the front panel.
- The front-left three-quarter face disappears.
- Body becomes tan, brown, beige, realistic cardboard, stretched carton, tray, bridge, machine, or anonymous package.
- Linework becomes thin, smooth, ruler-straight, auto-traced, thresholded, posterized, or clean vector.
- Littlebox becomes a smiling cute mascot, wears accessories, or looks like a delivery-company icon.
- Removing Littlebox would leave the core metaphor unchanged.

## Recognition Rule

Across transformations, preserve at least four markers:

- box body
- dot eyes
- front-left three-quarter view
- closed top flaps
- tiny legs
- side-seam twig arms when acting
- single amber seam tape with jagged front tab

For final article images, preserve at least five markers. Dot eyes, compact box body, closed lid, side-seam twig arms when acting, and jagged amber front tab are required when visible.
